The Solution

Bēhere
RE Village

"When we make education the heart of community, the village comes alive."

— Brooke Lawler, Co-Founder

The Bēhere RE Village is a living campus in Santa Barbara County where families don't just visit — they belong. "RE" stands for Rethinking Everything. It's a place where children learn by doing: growing food, building structures, performing on stage, coding tools, and sitting with elders around a fire.

Education here isn't a room you're put in — it's a landscape you move through. Every acre is a classroom. Every season is a curriculum. Every neighbor is a teacher.

How It Works

Nature-Rooted.
Project-Based.
Alive with the Seasons.

🍂 Fall

Entrepreneurship

The Harvest of Ideas

Children discover what they have to offer the world — crafting, selling, pitching, and building their first small ventures from the abundance of harvest.

❄️ Winter

Ancestral Wisdom

Root to Remember

Elders and story. Craft and fire. The slow season is for going deep — into history, heritage, and the ancient skills our grandparents carried.

🌸 Spring

Creativity

Rebirth and Renewal

Paint, build, perform, design. Spring is when children make things that didn't exist before — and discover who they are when they create.

☀️ Summer

Community Sufficiency

The Great Feast

Gardens in full bloom, tables overflowing, neighbors sharing abundance. Summer is the living proof that community can feed itself — in every sense.
